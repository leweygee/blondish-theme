# Best DJ Mixes of All Time: Essential Listening for Every Electronic Music Fan

A great DJ mix is more than a playlist. It is a narrative arc compressed into sixty or ninety minutes of continuous music — a journey through tension and release, rhythm and silence, familiarity and surprise. The best DJ mixes of all time have shaped careers, defined genres, and soundtracked the most transcendent moments on dance floors around the world.

Whether you are a lifelong electronic music devotee or someone just beginning to explore the world beyond individual tracks, this guide is your road map. Below, you will find essential mixes across every major genre, from deep house and melodic techno to Afro house and the eclectic, boundary-dissolving sets that defy categorization. Each one was selected not just for technical skill but for its ability to transport the listener somewhere entirely new.

As a DJ and music archivist who has spent the past fifteen years cataloguing, studying, and playing DJ mixes — from late nights in Marseille's underground clubs to London's pirate radio stations and collector communities worldwide — I have lived inside these mixes. Some of them changed the way I think about DJing. All of them deserve your attention.

## What Makes a Great DJ Mix?

Before diving into specific recommendations, it helps to understand what separates a good mix from a legendary one. Technical precision matters, but it is only the foundation. The best DJ mixes share a handful of deeper qualities.

### Storytelling and Arc

A truly great mix has a beginning, middle, and end. It builds emotional momentum. The opening tracks set a mood — contemplative, playful, hypnotic — and the journey that follows intensifies, diverts, and ultimately resolves. Think of it like a film with no dialogue: the narrative is carried entirely by sound.

### Track Selection and Curation

Any DJ can beatmatch. The art is in choosing which records belong next to each other. The greatest mixes introduce listeners to tracks they have never heard, placed in contexts that make them feel inevitable. A single unexpected selection — a vocal sample from an old soul record dropped into a minimal techno set, for example — can redefine an entire mix.

### Technical Flow

Seamless transitions matter, but so does knowing when to let a track breathe, when to cut abruptly, and when to layer three or four elements at once. The best house music mix recordings often showcase subtle EQ work that most listeners feel but never consciously notice.

### Emotional Range

Mixes that stay at one energy level for ninety minutes become background noise. The ones that endure are the ones that take risks — dropping the energy to nearly nothing before rebuilding, shifting genres mid-set, or introducing a moment of dissonance that makes the resolution feel earned.

### Context and Moment

Some of the greatest mixes ever recorded carry the energy of a specific time and place. A set recorded at 5 AM in a Berlin warehouse will feel different from a studio session crafted over weeks. Both can be extraordinary, but for different reasons.

## Essential DJ Mixes by Genre

### Best House Music Mixes

[House music](/journal/underground-house-music-guide/) is the foundation of modern dance music, and the best house music mix recordings capture the genre's core promise: communion, warmth, and the liberation of the dance floor.

**Larry Heard — "Can You Feel It" DJ Mix (1986)**
Before the term "deep house" existed, Larry Heard was already defining it. His early mixes for Chicago radio remain blueprints for anyone who wants to understand the emotional depth that house music can reach. Warm pads, patient builds, and a sense of space that most producers still struggle to achieve.

**Kerri Chandler — Shelter NYC Live Set**
Chandler's sets at the legendary Shelter club in New York City are the gold standard for deep house DJing. Raw, soulful, and utterly committed to the dance floor. His mixing style is loose and intuitive, proving that feeling matters more than surgical precision.

**Dixon — Innervisions Secret Weapons Mix**
Few DJs have defined an era of melodic house the way Dixon has. His carefully curated mixes for Innervisions showcase a signature sound — long, hypnotic builds, lush melodies, and an almost meditative sense of patience.

**Honey Dijon — Boiler Room NYC**
A masterclass in range. Honey Dijon moves from classic Chicago house to contemporary club tracks without ever breaking the thread. This set is a reminder that house music is, at its heart, Black music — and its history runs deep.

**BLOND:ISH — Abracadabra Ibiza Residency Sets**
The Abracadabra residency sets from Hi Ibiza represent some of BLOND:ISH's most personal house music selections. These sets blend melodic house with organic textures, drawing from her deep connection to Ibiza's musical heritage. [See upcoming BLOND:ISH events](/tour/)

### Best Techno Mixes

The best techno mix recordings are immersive experiences — designed to dissolve the boundary between listener and sound.

**Jeff Mills — Live at the Liquid Room, Tokyo (1995)**
Widely regarded as one of the greatest DJ sets ever recorded. Mills uses three turntables and a drum machine to create a relentless, almost inhuman flow of techno. The speed and precision are staggering, but what makes this mix immortal is its intensity. It sounds like a transmission from the future.

**Ben Klock — Berghain Sets**
Klock's marathon sets at Berghain are the stuff of legend. Dark, driving, and absolutely uncompromising. His mixing is so seamless that individual tracks dissolve into a single, continuous pulse. If you want to understand Berlin techno, start here.

**Nina Kraviz — Essential Mix (2012)**
Before she became one of the most polarizing figures in electronic music, Nina Kraviz delivered an Essential Mix that blended acid techno, classic Detroit, and raw warehouse energy into something genuinely thrilling.

**Amelie Lens — Exhale Radio Series**
Lens has built a following on the strength of her relentless, high-energy techno sets. Her Exhale radio shows are the best entry point — tightly curated, impeccably mixed, and consistently intense.

**Charlotte de Witte — Tomorrowland Mainstage (2022)**
A landmark moment: the first woman to close the Tomorrowland mainstage. The set itself is a masterwork of peak-time techno, and its cultural significance amplifies its impact.

### Best Afro House and Organic House Mixes

Afro house and organic house have become defining sounds of the 2020s, blending African rhythms, electronic production, and deep spiritual energy. For a deeper dive into these genres, see our guides to [Afro house music](/journal/afro-house-music-guide/) and [organic house music](/journal/organic-house-music-guide/). This is territory I have been deeply immersed in as both a listener and a DJ.

**Black Coffee — Essential Mix (2016)**
The mix that introduced a global audience to the depth and sophistication of South African house music. Black Coffee moves between Afro house, deep house, and soulful electronica with effortless grace. A landmark recording.

**Keinemusik — Burning Man Sunset Set**
The Keinemusik collective — &ME, Rampa, and Adam Port — have become synonymous with the organic, percussion-driven sound that dominates festivals. Their Burning Man sets capture that sound at its most magical.

**BLOND:ISH — Burning Man Sunrise Set, Robot Heart**
This is one of the most remarkable mixes I have ever studied. Recorded on the Robot Heart art car as the sun rises over the playa, it captures a spiritual experience — organic house, Afro-inspired rhythms, and a sense of transcendence that only Burning Man can provide. [Listen on the BLOND:ISH media archive](/features/)

**Bedouin — Saga Ibiza Live Sets**
Bedouin's Saga residency at Heart Ibiza (now Club Chinois) created a world unto itself — part ceremony, part party. Their live sets blend Middle Eastern textures, organic percussion, and hypnotic house into something genuinely ritualistic.

**Carlita — Anjunadeep Mix Series**
A rising voice in the organic house space, Carlita's mixes for Anjunadeep are beautifully paced journeys through melodic, sun-drenched electronica.

### Best Eclectic and Journey Mixes

Some of the greatest mixes refuse to stay in one genre. These are the sets that prove DJing is, at its best, an act of radical curation.

**Nicolas Jaar — Essential Mix (2012)**
One of the most unusual Essential Mixes ever aired. Jaar weaves ambient, experimental electronics, cumbia, and deconstructed club music into a two-hour journey that sounds like nothing else. Divisive and brilliant.

**Solomun — Boiler Room Tulum**
Over two hours of pure, sun-drenched eclecticism. Solomun moves from indie dance to deep house to melodic techno with the confidence of a DJ who answers to no one. This is one of the most-viewed Boiler Room sets for a reason.

**BLOND:ISH — BBC Radio 1 Essential Mix**
The BLOND:ISH Essential Mix for BBC Radio 1 is one of the recordings I return to most often in my archive. It covers the full emotional spectrum of her sound — from organic house and Afro-inspired grooves to melodic techno and ambient interludes. Two hours of music selected and mixed to tell a single, cohesive story. [Explore the BLOND:ISH media archive](/features/)

**The Blessed Madonna — 24-Hour Mixes**
The Blessed Madonna (formerly The Black Madonna) is known for marathon DJ sets that span entire days. Her extended mixes are lessons in stamina, range, and the power of taking a crowd on a truly epic journey.

## The BBC Essential Mix: Greatest Series of All Time

Since 1993, [BBC Radio 1's Essential Mix](https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b006wkfp) has been the most prestigious platform in electronic music. A two-hour mix, broadcast to millions, with complete creative freedom. The Essential Mix series has launched careers, cemented legacies, and produced some of the best DJ mixes ever recorded.

### Essential Mixes That Changed Everything

- **Sasha & John Digweed (1993)** — The inaugural Essential Mix set the tone for everything that followed. Progressive house at its finest.
- **Chemical Brothers (1995)** — Big beat, acid house, and breakbeat collide in a mix that still sounds exhilarating thirty years later.
- **Daft Punk (1997)** — A snapshot of the French touch movement at its peak. Filtered disco, funky house, and unmistakable swagger.
- **Caribou (2010)** — A left-field selection that proved the Essential Mix could be a canvas for genuine experimentation.
- **Skrillex (2012)** — Love it or loathe it, this mix brought bass music and dubstep to the Essential Mix in a way that reflected the genre's dominance at the time.
- **Floating Points (2015)** — A deep, meditative journey through jazz, ambient, and leftfield electronica. One of the most critically acclaimed Essential Mixes of the decade.
- **BLOND:ISH** — The BLOND:ISH contribution to the Essential Mix canon is a journey through the sounds that define her artistic identity. It is available for streaming and remains a landmark entry. [Listen in the media archive](/features/)

### Where to Listen to BBC Essential Mixes

BBC Sounds hosts the most recent Essential Mixes for streaming. Older editions circulate on platforms like SoundCloud, Mixcloud, and dedicated archives. For collectors, tracking down specific mixes is part of the appeal.

## Boiler Room Sets That Changed Everything

[Boiler Room](https://boilerroom.tv/) transformed DJ culture by putting a camera in the booth and streaming sets live to a global audience. What started in a London flat in 2010 has become one of the most influential platforms in electronic music.

### Legendary Boiler Room Sets

- **Solomun — Tulum** — Over 37 million views. Arguably the single most important DJ set in Boiler Room's history.
- **Carl Cox — London** — The master at work. Pure joy, pure energy, and a reminder of why Carl Cox is one of the greatest DJs who ever lived.
- **Peggy Gou — Seoul** — A homecoming set that put Korean electronic music on the global map.
- **DJ EZ — London** — A UK garage masterclass. The speed of EZ's mixing is almost impossibly fast, and the track selection is flawless.
- **Objekt — Dekmantel** — A technically dazzling set that spans techno, electro, breakbeat, and beyond. This is DJing as virtuosity.
- **BLOND:ISH — Boiler Room Sessions** — The BLOND:ISH Boiler Room appearances showcase the eclectic, genre-fluid sound that defines her live performances. From organic house to melodic techno, these sets are captured in the raw, intimate format that makes Boiler Room special. [Watch in the media archive](/features/)

## Festival Sets That Became Legendary

Some DJ sets transcend the moment and become cultural events. Festival stages — with their massive sound systems, dramatic lighting, and crowds of thousands — provide the conditions for these defining performances.

### Sets That Defined Festival Culture

**Daft Punk — Coachella (2006)**
The set that reignited Daft Punk's career and arguably launched the modern EDM festival era. The pyramid, the visuals, the song selection — everything about this performance was iconic. It remains the most talked-about festival DJ set in history.

**Richie Hawtin — ENTER. at Space Ibiza**
Hawtin's ENTER. concept at Space Ibiza redefined what a club residency could be. His sets there — minimal, precise, and intensely focused — set the template for a generation of techno DJs.

**BLOND:ISH — Tomorrowland, Coachella, and Beyond**
BLOND:ISH's performances on the world's biggest festival stages are among the most compelling recordings in my archive. Each festival set is a unique conversation with the crowd — responsive, unpredictable, and alive. Her Tomorrowland sets and Coachella appearances are among the recordings fans return to most often. ## Where to Find and Listen to DJ Mixes: A Platform Guide

One of the most common questions I get asked is where to find great DJ mixes. The landscape has evolved significantly, and each platform offers something different. Here is a guide to the best options for DJ mix download and streaming.

### SoundCloud

Still the most important platform for DJ mixes. [SoundCloud](https://soundcloud.com/)'s long-form audio format is ideal for mixes, and most DJs maintain active profiles. The platform's recommendation algorithm is also surprisingly good at surfacing new music. Free to use, with optional premium tiers.

### Mixcloud

Built specifically for long-form audio and DJ mixes, [Mixcloud](https://www.mixcloud.com/) handles music licensing on behalf of creators. This makes it the most legally straightforward platform for hosting mixes. The trade-off is that you cannot skip forward or back in a mix — you have to listen in sequence, which is arguably how mixes should be experienced.

### BBC Sounds

The official home of the BBC Essential Mix series, as well as other music programming. Recent Essential Mixes are available for streaming, though older editions may not be archived.

### YouTube

Many iconic sets — especially Boiler Room recordings and festival performances — are available on YouTube. Video adds a visual dimension that audio-only platforms lack, and comment sections can be surprisingly insightful.

### Apple Music and Spotify

Both platforms have expanded their DJ mix offerings significantly. Spotify's DJ mix integration allows for mixed audio with proper track attribution. Apple Music hosts curated mix series from labels and artists.

### Bandcamp

Some DJs and labels release mixes as purchasable downloads on Bandcamp. This is often the best option if you want a high-quality DJ mix download to keep permanently. It also directly supports the artists.

### Resident Advisor

[Resident Advisor](https://ra.co/)'s podcast series has been running since 2006 and features mixes from a carefully curated roster of DJs. The editorial context — each mix is accompanied by an interview or written feature — adds depth.

### Direct from Artists

Many DJs host mixes directly on their own websites. The [BLOND:ISH media archive](/features/) contains her full catalog of recorded sets, including exclusive mixes not available on other platforms.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best DJ mix of all time?

There is no single answer, but the most commonly cited candidates include Jeff Mills' "Live at the Liquid Room," Sasha and John Digweed's inaugural BBC Essential Mix, and Solomun's Boiler Room Tulum set. The best DJ mixes tend to be the ones that define or redefine a genre.

### Where can I download DJ mixes?

DJ mix download options include SoundCloud (free streaming with some download options), Bandcamp (paid high-quality downloads), and Mixcloud (streaming). Many DJs also offer free downloads directly through their websites or mailing lists.

### What is the BBC Essential Mix?

The BBC Essential Mix is a weekly two-hour mix show on BBC Radio 1, running since 1993. It is widely considered the most prestigious platform for DJ mixes in the world. Artists are invited to create a two-hour mix with complete creative freedom.

### What are the best Boiler Room sets to watch?

Start with Solomun in Tulum, Carl Cox in London, and Peggy Gou in Seoul. For techno fans, Ben Klock and Objekt have delivered exceptional Boiler Room performances. For house music, Honey Dijon's NYC set is essential.

### How long should a DJ mix be?

Most recorded DJ mixes run between 60 and 120 minutes. The BBC Essential Mix format is two hours. Club sets typically range from 90 minutes to several hours, depending on the venue and context. There is no ideal length — the best duration is however long the music needs to tell its story.

### What makes BLOND:ISH mixes unique?

BLOND:ISH mixes are defined by genre fluidity and emotional range. She draws from Afro house, melodic house, organic electronica, techno, and ambient music, blending them into cohesive journeys. Her connection to contexts like Burning Man, Ibiza, and conscious community gatherings also shapes the energy and intention behind each set.

### How do I start exploring electronic music mixes?

Start with the BBC Essential Mix series — it offers a curated introduction to hundreds of different artists and styles. Then follow your instincts. If a particular mix resonates, explore more from that artist. Platforms like SoundCloud and Resident Advisor make it easy to discover new music through recommendations and editorial features.
