# Music Festival Packing List: The Complete & Sustainable Guide

**After attending festivals on five continents — from Burning Man's alkali desert to the jungles of Tulum, from muddy UK fields to the beaches of Bali — I have refined my packing list to the essentials. Everything below has earned its place through direct experience. Nothing is filler. And wherever possible, I've included the sustainable alternative, because your festival footprint matters.**

*By Layla Kardan — March 2026*

---

## The Philosophy: Pack Less, Choose Better

The best festival-goer is the one who arrives prepared without being weighed down. Every item you bring needs to earn its place. The heaviest thing in your bag should be your water — not gear you never unpack.

This guide is organized by category with a baseline essential list, sustainable swaps, and notes on what to skip.

---

## The Essential Festival Packing List

### Shelter & Sleep

| Item | Why | Sustainable Option |
|------|-----|-------------------|
| **Tent** (if camping) | Obviously | Buy quality, reuse for years — never abandon a tent (250,000 go to UK landfill annually) |
| **Sleeping bag** | Rated for the climate | Synthetic recycled-fill bags from brands like Vaude or Sea to Summit |
| **Sleeping pad/mat** | Ground insulation and comfort | Self-inflating pad, reusable for years |
| **Earplugs for sleeping** | Camps are loud at 4 AM | Reusable silicone or wax earplugs |
| **Eye mask** | Sleep after sunrise | Any comfortable mask — you'll use it for years |
| **Pillow** | Compressible camp pillow | Stuff sack filled with clothes works too |

**Skip:** Air mattresses (heavy, fragile), camping chairs (you won't use them as much as you think).

### Clothing

The golden rule: **layers**. Festivals swing between extremes — blazing sun to freezing night, dust storms to rain. Pack for versatility, not fashion.

| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| **Lightweight layers** (2-3 tops) | Quick-dry, breathable fabrics |
| **Warm layer** (fleece or down jacket) | Essential for night/morning |
| **Rain jacket** (if applicable) | Packable, waterproof, not a poncho (ponchos are waste) |
| **Comfortable shoes** (broken-in) | Boots for muddy festivals, trainers for dry |
| **Sandals/flip-flops** | For camp and showers only |
| **Sunhat/cap** | Non-negotiable for daytime |
| **Bandana/buff** | Dust protection, sun cover, style |
| **Swimwear** (if water is available) | One piece is enough |
| **Socks** (3-4 pairs, merino) | Merino wool is antimicrobial and quick-drying — the single best festival fabric |
| **Underwear** (3-4) | Merino or quick-dry synthetic |

**Sustainable note:** Buy secondhand festival clothing from Depop, Vinted, or charity shops. The fast-fashion festival outfit you wear once has a significant environmental cost. [Read about sustainability in music](/journal/sustainability-music-industry/)

**Skip:** Sequin outfits (microplastics), excessive costume changes, new shoes (blisters guaranteed).

### Hydration & Nutrition

| Item | Why |
|------|-----|
| **Reusable water bottle (1L minimum)** | The single most important item on this list. Refill stations are standard at most festivals now |
| **Electrolyte sachets** | Dehydration prevention — far more effective than water alone |
| **Snack bars/trail mix** | Energy between meals, no vendor queuing |
| **Reusable cutlery set** | Bamboo or titanium — eliminates single-use utensil waste |
| **Reusable cup/mug** | Many festivals now require or incentivize reusable cups |

**Sustainable note:** [Bye Bye Plastic](/projects/bye-bye-plastic/) has been instrumental in pushing festivals toward reusable cup systems and refill stations. Support festivals that have taken the pledge.

**Skip:** Disposable water bottles, single-use cutlery, excessive snacks (festivals have food vendors for a reason).

### Health & Safety

| Item | Why |
|------|-----|
| **Sunscreen (SPF 50)** | Mineral/reef-safe formula preferred |
| **Concert earplugs** | Filtered earplugs ([Etymotic](https://www.etymotic.com/), [Loop](https://www.loopearplugs.com/), EarPeace) — protect hearing while preserving sound quality. This is non-negotiable. |
| **Basic first aid** | Plasters, antiseptic, blister pads, painkillers, antihistamines |
| **Prescription medications** | Enough for the full trip plus extras |
| **Hand sanitizer** | Portable, non-aerosol |
| **Toilet paper/tissue packs** | Festivals run out. You won't. |
| **Wet wipes** (biodegradable) | For when showers aren't available |
| **Insect repellent** | Essential for jungle/forest/lakeside festivals |
| **Menstrual products** | Pack extra — your preferred type plus backup |

**Skip:** Full makeup kits (dust/sweat/rain will destroy it), perfume/cologne (attracts insects, pointless outdoors).

### Tech & Power

| Item | Why |
|------|-----|
| **Phone + portable charger** | Fully charged. Airplane mode saves battery dramatically |
| **Solar charger** | For multi-day camping festivals — a worthwhile investment |
| **Headlamp** | Far more useful than a phone flashlight. Red light mode preserves night vision |
| **Small padlock** | For tent zippers and lockers |
| **Cash + card** | Many festivals are cashless now, but always carry some cash as backup |
| **Photo ID** | Required for entry and age verification |
| **Printed tickets/confirmation** | Phone batteries die. Paper doesn't |

**Skip:** Laptop, large camera (unless you're a photographer), Bluetooth speakers (you're at a festival — there's already music).

## Festival-Type Specific Add-Ons

### Camping Festivals (UK, European, US)
- Wellington boots (UK festivals — non-negotiable)
- Tarp/groundsheet under tent
- Camping lantern (solar-powered)
- Dry bags for valuables in rain
- Small towel (microfiber, quick-dry)

### Desert Festivals ([Burning Man](/journal/burning-man-music-guide/), AfrikaBurn)
- Goggles (dust storms)
- Dust mask/N95
- Heavy-duty sunscreen
- Electrolytes (double what you think you need)
- Bicycle + lights
- Extra water capacity (Burning Man has NO vendors)

### Tropical/Jungle Festivals (Tulum, Bali, Costa Rica)
- Insect repellent (DEET or natural alternative — both work)
- Light, long-sleeved shirt (mosquito protection at dusk)
- Reef-safe sunscreen (non-negotiable near ocean)
- Waterproof bag for electronics (humidity)

### Day Festivals (No Camping)
Simplified kit — just the essentials:
- Reusable water bottle
- Earplugs
- Sunscreen + hat
- Portable charger
- Cash + card + ID
- Light jacket (for evening)
- Comfortable shoes

## The Sustainable Festival Checklist

Every choice compounds. Here are the swaps that make the biggest environmental difference:

1. **Reusable water bottle** instead of buying plastic bottles (saves ~15 bottles per festival)
2. **Reusable cup** instead of single-use cups (saves ~20 cups per festival)
3. **Secondhand/existing clothing** instead of fast-fashion festival outfits
4. **Public transport, shuttle, or carpool** instead of driving solo (transport is 60-80% of a festival's carbon footprint)
5. **Biodegradable wet wipes** instead of standard wipes (standard wipes contain plastic)
6. **Mineral sunscreen** instead of chemical sunscreen (protects waterways)
7. **Take your tent home** instead of abandoning it
8. **Leave no trace** — pack out everything you pack in

**The single biggest impact:** How you get there. One person carpooling vs driving solo cuts transport emissions by 50-75%. Taking a festival shuttle or train cuts them further. [Learn more about sustainability in the music industry](/journal/sustainability-music-industry/)

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What should I pack for a music festival?

The essentials for any music festival: reusable water bottle, concert earplugs (hearing protection), sunscreen, comfortable broken-in shoes, layers for temperature changes, portable phone charger, ID, and cash/card. For camping festivals, add a quality tent, sleeping bag, sleeping pad, headlamp, and toilet paper. Always bring earplugs — hearing damage from festivals is cumulative and irreversible.

### What is the most important thing to bring to a festival?

A reusable water bottle and concert earplugs. Dehydration is the most common health issue at festivals, and hearing damage is the most common long-term consequence. Everything else is secondary to keeping yourself hydrated and protecting your hearing. High-quality filtered earplugs (Etymotic, Loop) reduce volume by 15-25 dB while preserving sound quality.

### How do I pack sustainably for a festival?

Use reusable alternatives (water bottle, cup, cutlery), choose secondhand or existing clothing over fast-fashion festival outfits, use biodegradable wet wipes and mineral sunscreen, carpool or use public transport/shuttles, and never abandon your tent or camping gear. The biggest single impact is transport — how you get to the festival accounts for 60-80% of its carbon footprint.

### What should I not bring to a music festival?

Skip: glass containers (banned at most festivals), large amounts of cash, expensive jewelry, new unworn shoes (blisters), full-size toiletries (decant into travel sizes), heavy camping chairs, inflatable mattresses, and Bluetooth speakers. Also skip single-use items like disposable plates, cutlery, and water bottles — bring reusable alternatives.

### How many outfits should I pack for a festival?

For a 3-day festival: 2-3 tops, 1-2 bottoms, 3-4 pairs of socks and underwear (merino wool if possible), one warm layer, and one rain layer. Festival clothing gets dirty — pack for comfort and practicality, not outfit changes. Layers are more valuable than variety.
